Festival of the Medina in Tunis

History Experience Gallery Location Travel Tips

Every year during Ramadan, the UNESCO‑listed Medina of Tunis turns into a cultural stage of music, dance, storytelling, and art. This festival invites visitors to enjoy Tunisia’s heritage in a vibrant, welcoming atmosphere.

History and Significance

Established in the 1980s, the festival revitalises Tunisia’s artistic traditions within historic mosques, palaces, and courtyards after sunset prayers, turning the Medina into a living museum.

What to Experience

Enjoy classical Malouf music, folklore theatre, poetry, exhibitions and lantern-lit evenings filled with traditional food aromas. It's family-friendly and immersive.

Gallery

Festival Location – Medina of Tunis

The events take place across the UNESCO World Heritage–listed Medina of Tunis, in historic alleys and courtyards.

Travel Tips

Visit during Ramadan; events start after sunset. Many are free; special palace shows might have tickets. Explore souks and enjoy local iftar cuisine. The Medina is walkable but wear comfy shoes.
